What is the inventory conversion period?
the inventory conversion period is a measurement of the average number of days the inventory is held. It is also called the days in inventory.
How to calculate inventory conversion period?
To calculate inventory conversion period you should use the following formula:
inventory conversion period = (inventory / sales) * 365
example: a company has an inventory of $4,000,000 and the average daily sales are $42,000, calculate the inventory conversion period.
first let's calculate the yearly sales:
yearly sales = daily sales * 365
yearly sales = $42,000 * 365
yearly sales = $15,330,000
now we can calculate the inventory conversion period:
inventory conversion period = (inventory / sales) * 365
inventory conversion period = ($4,000,000 / $15,330,000) * 365
inventory conversion period = 95 days
Why use inventory conversion period?
conversion inventory period is used to assess the average cash cycle and manage it better. Also it helps the company purchase management and to decide if the inventory should be purchased or manufacured.
